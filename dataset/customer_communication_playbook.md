## Purpose

This playbook establishes the standards, procedures, approved language, and templates that govern all customer-facing communications issued by abc.co during operational incidents. It applies whenever a customer experiences or is at risk of experiencing a service disruption, including delayed shipments, stockouts, fulfillment exceptions, and order modifications.

The purpose of this playbook is to ensure that:

- Customers receive accurate, timely, and professional communications during incidents
- The company presents a consistent, accountable voice across all channels and team members
- No unauthorized commitments — including refunds, credits, or delivery guarantees — are made to customers
- Customer communications are fully documented and traceable to their source incident
- Communication standards are aligned with the escalation rules defined in the Shipment Delay Escalation SOP (ABC-OPS-SOP-004)

This playbook is the authoritative reference for all Customer Success Team members handling customer communication during operational incidents. Adherence is mandatory.

---

## Scope

This playbook applies to all customer-facing communication issued by the Customer Success Team, Account Managers, and Operations Coordinators in a customer-facing capacity, covering:

- Enterprise accounts (Tier 1 and Tier 2 CRM classification)
- Standard accounts (non-enterprise recurring customers)
- Branch-level customers
- Recurring supply customers on standing orders

**Communication types covered:**

- Outbound proactive communications during shipment delays
- Responses to inbound customer inquiries about order status
- Stockout and fulfillment exception notifications
- Revised ETA confirmations
- Service credit review acknowledgments

**Out of scope:**

- Marketing and promotional communications (governed by Marketing communications policy)
- Contract negotiation and renewal communications (managed by Account Management)
- Legal dispute correspondence (managed by Legal and Senior Management)

## Communication Principles

All customer communications during operational incidents must adhere to the following principles:

1. **Be factual and specific.** State what is known, what has happened, and what the next action is. Avoid vague language such as "there may be a delay" when the delay is confirmed.

2. **Do not overpromise.** Commitments on delivery timelines, compensation, or resolution must be confirmed by the relevant authority before being communicated. A Customer Success representative may not make commitments that are not yet authorized.

3. **Do not assign blame to internal teams or courier partners.** Customer communications must not identify specific internal teams, warehouse staff, or carrier companies as responsible for a failure. Refer to "a logistics disruption," "a fulfillment delay," or "an issue at our end" as appropriate.

4. **Use approved templates for high-severity incidents.** For delays exceeding 24 hours, stockout notifications, and service credit acknowledgments, approved templates must be used. Minor wording adjustments are permitted; substantive changes require Customer Success Manager review.

5. **Escalate financial compensation requests to Finance.** Any customer request for a refund, credit, or financial remedy must be logged and routed to the Finance Team. Customer Success personnel must not communicate a specific credit amount until Finance approval is obtained.

6. **Confirm revised ETA before sharing.** No revised delivery timeline may be communicated to a customer unless it has been confirmed by the Logistics Manager or explicitly approved by the Branch Operations Lead.

## Approved Communication Channels

| Channel | Usage | Response Standard |
|---|---|---|
| **Email** | Primary channel for proactive updates and formal communications for all account types | Within 4 hours of incident classification (for proactive outbound) |
| **Customer Portal** | Incident ticket updates and order status notes for enterprise accounts | Updated within 2 hours of any material change |
| **Phone Follow-Up** | For enterprise accounts (Tier 1) and customer-initiated inquiries during Severity 3 and 4 escalations | Return call within 1 hour of missed call; attempted within same business day |
| **Account Manager Update** | Direct communication from named Account Manager to customer counterpart for critical accounts | As required per account tier and incident severity |
| **Incident Ticket Comment** | Internal and customer-visible notes within the order management or ticketing system | Updated with every status change; minimum once per 6 hours during active escalation |

All communications must be directed through one of the channels listed above. Informal channels (personal messaging apps, personal email, verbal commitments without follow-up documentation) are not permitted for incident communications.

## Shipment Delay Communication Rules

Customer communication obligations vary based on the duration of the shipment delay. The following rules are aligned with the severity classification in the Shipment Delay Escalation SOP (ABC-OPS-SOP-004):

| Delay Duration | Customer Communication Requirement | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Under 12 hours | No proactive outbound communication required unless the customer has already contacted support | Operations Coordinator (if inquiry received) |
| 12–24 hours | Customer Success may send a brief, factual status update if the delay is expected to approach or exceed 24 hours | Customer Success Team |
| Over 24 hours | Proactive customer communication is **required**. Must include: acknowledgment of delay, factual description, and confirmed revised ETA | Customer Success Manager |
| Over 48 hours | Formal written communication is **required** via email or customer portal. Must use the approved template for delays over 48 hours | Customer Success Manager |
| Over 72 hours | Account Manager or Customer Success Manager review required for enterprise accounts. A resolution plan must be communicated | Account Manager (enterprise); Customer Success Manager (standard) |

## Refund and Credit Communication Rules

The following rules govern all customer-facing communications related to financial remediation:

- **Customer Success cannot promise refunds directly.** No Customer Success representative is authorized to tell a customer that a refund will be issued. The permissible language is: "We can review this situation and assess whether a service remedy is appropriate."

- **Service credits require Finance approval.** A service credit cannot be confirmed to a customer until the Finance Team has issued approval per the applicable threshold.

- **Credits up to ₹5,000** may be reviewed and approved by the Finance Team without escalation. Customer Success initiates the review request.

- **Credits between ₹5,001 and ₹25,000** require Finance Manager approval before the credit amount is communicated to the customer.

- **Credits above ₹25,000** require COO approval before communication.

- **Approved language for credit inquiry acknowledgment:** "We have noted your concern and have initiated a review of your account for possible service remediation. We will follow up with you within [X] business days with an update." The number of business days stated must be realistic and agreed with the Finance Team.

- **Unapproved language:** Do not say "You will receive a credit," "We will refund you," "You are entitled to compensation," or any language that implies a financial commitment has already been authorized.

## Tone Guidelines

All customer communications must reflect the following tone standards:

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Professional** | Use formal written English. Avoid slang, colloquialisms, or casual phrasing. |
| **Accountable** | Acknowledge the impact of the incident on the customer. Do not minimize or deflect. |
| **Clear** | Use plain language. Avoid internal jargon (e.g., do not reference SOP numbers, internal ticket IDs, or team names in customer-facing messages). |
| **Non-defensive** | Do not explain the delay in a way that implies the customer's expectations were unreasonable. |
| **Time-bound** | Where a next action is committed, specify when it will occur (e.g., "We will provide an update by end of business today"). |
| **Non-blame-shifting** | Do not attribute the incident to specific internal teams or carrier partners by name. |

---

## Words and Phrases to Avoid

The following phrases are prohibited in customer-facing communications:

| Prohibited Phrase | Reason |
|---|---|
| "The courier messed up" | Assigns blame to a third party; unprofessional |
| "This is not our fault" | Defensive; damages customer trust |
| "You will definitely receive a refund" | Unauthorized financial commitment |
| "The warehouse team failed to dispatch" | Blames internal team; not appropriate for external communication |
| "I promise delivery today" | Only permitted if ETA is confirmed by Logistics Manager |
| "The system was down" | Vague and unhelpful; does not instill confidence |
| "We are doing our best" | Non-specific; implies inadequate capability |
| "It's stuck in customs" | Irrelevant for domestic shipments; confuses the customer |

## Approved Templates

### Template 1: Delay Under 24 Hours (Status Update)

> **Subject:** Order Update — Order #[ORDER_ID]
>
> Dear [Customer Name / Contact Name],
>
> We are writing to inform you that your order #[ORDER_ID] is experiencing a short delay in delivery. We expect your shipment to arrive by [REVISED ETA — confirmed by Logistics].
>
> We apologize for any inconvenience this may cause and are actively working to ensure your order reaches you as quickly as possible.
>
> If you have any questions or require further assistance, please do not hesitate to contact us.
>
> Warm regards,
> [Customer Success Representative Name]
> Customer Success Team, abc.co

---

### Template 2: Delay Over 24 Hours (Proactive Update)

> **Subject:** Important Update Regarding Your Order #[ORDER_ID]
>
> Dear [Customer Name / Contact Name],
>
> We want to proactively inform you that your order #[ORDER_ID], originally scheduled for delivery on [ORIGINAL DELIVERY DATE], has been delayed due to [brief factual description, e.g., "a logistics disruption affecting deliveries in your region"].
>
> We have confirmed a revised estimated delivery date of **[REVISED ETA]**. Our team is closely monitoring your shipment to ensure it reaches you without further delay.
>
> We sincerely apologize for this disruption to your operations. Should you require any urgent assistance or have questions about specific items in your order, please contact your Customer Success representative at [CONTACT DETAILS].
>
> Warm regards,
> [Customer Success Manager Name]
> Customer Success Team, abc.co

---

### Template 3: Delay Over 48 Hours (Formal Written Communication)

> **Subject:** Formal Update — Significant Delivery Delay for Order #[ORDER_ID]
>
> Dear [Customer Name / Contact Name],
>
> We are writing to formally notify you of a significant delay affecting your order #[ORDER_ID], which was scheduled for delivery on [ORIGINAL DELIVERY DATE]. As of today, [CURRENT DATE], your shipment has not yet been delivered.
>
> **Current Status:** [Brief factual status, e.g., "Your shipment is in transit and has been re-routed to address the disruption."]
>
> **Revised Estimated Delivery Date:** [REVISED ETA, confirmed by Logistics Team]
>
> We acknowledge the impact this delay may have on your operations and take full responsibility for ensuring your order is delivered at the earliest opportunity. Our Customer Success and Logistics teams are prioritizing your shipment and will provide you with a further update no later than [NEXT UPDATE DATE/TIME].
>
> If you would like to discuss this matter further or require interim arrangements, please contact [CUSTOMER SUCCESS MANAGER NAME] directly at [CONTACT DETAILS].
>
> We sincerely apologize for this disruption.
>
> Yours sincerely,
> [Customer Success Manager Name]
> Customer Success Team, abc.co

## Escalation to Account Manager

Account Manager involvement is required in the following situations:

- The affected account is classified as Tier 1 (critical account) and the delay exceeds 24 hours
- The customer has sent a formal written complaint or threatened to terminate a supply contract
- A service credit review has been initiated for an amount above ₹10,000
- The delay exceeds 72 hours for any enterprise account
- The customer has contacted senior management at abc.co directly
- Two or more incidents have been logged for the same customer within a 30-day period

When Account Manager involvement is triggered, the Customer Success Manager must brief the Account Manager within 1 hour of the trigger event, providing: Order ID, incident ticket ID, delay duration, customer communication history, and any credit review status.

## Documentation Requirements

Every customer communication issued during an operational incident must be logged in the incident management system immediately after sending. The following fields are mandatory:

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Customer Account ID | Must match CRM record |
| Order ID | Must match order in order management system |
| Incident or Ticket ID | Linked to the relevant escalation ticket |
| Timestamp | Date and time of communication (IST) |
| Communication Channel | Email, portal, phone, or other (specify) |
| Message Summary | Brief description of content communicated (50–100 words) |
| Revised ETA Communicated (if applicable) | Exact date and time shared with customer |
| Credit or Remedy Mentioned (if applicable) | Yes/No; if yes, note the language used and whether Finance approval was confirmed |
| Next Action Owner | Name and role of the person responsible for the next customer touchpoint |

Communications that are not logged within 2 hours of sending will be flagged in the weekly Customer Success audit review.

## Examples

### Example 1: Standard Customer — 15-Hour Delay

A standard account (Westfield Office Supplies) contacts Customer Success inquiring about an order that was due yesterday. The Operations Coordinator confirms a 15-hour delay due to a carrier vehicle breakdown.

- Customer Success uses Template 1 (Delay Under 24 Hours) with the revised ETA confirmed by the Logistics Manager.
- Communication is logged against the Order ID and incident ticket.
- No service credit is offered or implied.

### Example 2: Enterprise Customer — 36-Hour Delay with Credit Request

BluePeak Workspaces (Tier 2 enterprise account) emails requesting to know when their order will arrive and mentions they may need to seek compensation. The delay is 36 hours due to a dispatch backlog at Delhi.

- Customer Success Manager sends Template 2 (Delay Over 24 Hours) with the confirmed revised ETA.
- In response to the compensation inquiry, Customer Success Manager uses Template 6 language: "We have initiated a service review and will follow up within 2 business days."
- Finance Team is notified of potential credit review. Account Manager is briefed per escalation rules.
- No credit amount is communicated until Finance Manager approves.

### Example 3: Critical Account — 54-Hour Delay with Formal Communication

Meridian Clinics (Tier 1, critical account) has a 54-hour delay. The Account Manager is already engaged. Customer Success Manager sends Template 3 (Formal Written Communication) and coordinates with the Account Manager on the resolution plan. Credit review has been approved by Finance Manager at ₹12,000. Template 6 is sent first (review acknowledgment); once approved by Finance, a separate credit confirmation email is sent.
