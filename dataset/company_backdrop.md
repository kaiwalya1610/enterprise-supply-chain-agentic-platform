## Company Overview

**abc.co** is a mid-sized B2B operations and supply chain company headquartered in India. The company supplies a curated range of workplace and facility essentials to businesses that require reliable, high-volume recurring procurement without managing their own supply chains. abc.co's value proposition is operational reliability: predictable delivery schedules, consolidated vendor management, and account-managed customer relationships.

**Customer segments served:**

- Coworking space operators (managed workspaces, hot-desking facilities, enterprise-grade coworking)
- Boutique hotels and hospitality groups
- Clinics, diagnostic centers, and outpatient facilities
- Regional and branch offices of mid-to-large enterprises
- Facility management companies managing multiple client sites

**Product categories:**

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Office Supplies | Paper, pens, files, binders, toners, staplers |
| Pantry Goods | Coffee, tea, packaged snacks, sugar, disposable cups |
| Cleaning Supplies | Floor cleaners, disinfectants, mops, bins, gloves |
| Small Electronics | Keyboards, mice, USB hubs, cables, webcams, desk lamps |
| Ergonomic Furniture | Chairs, monitor stands, footrests, desk organizers |
| Facility Consumables | Toilet rolls, hand wash, paper towels, air fresheners, bin liners |

abc.co operates on a B2B recurring supply model. The majority of revenue is generated through monthly or quarterly supply agreements with enterprise and institutional customers. Spot orders from non-contracted customers constitute approximately 20–25% of order volume. Contracts are renewed annually and typically include agreed product catalogs, pricing tiers, and delivery frequency commitments.

## Branch Network

abc.co operates from four regional branches. Each branch functions as a fulfillment and customer operations hub for its zone.

| Branch | Region Served | Primary Responsibilities | Main Teams | Operational Risks |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mumbai** | West zone (Maharashtra, Gujarat, Goa) | Primary warehouse and fulfillment hub; west-zone order processing and dispatch | Operations, Logistics, Inventory Planning, Procurement | Port congestion, monsoon disruptions, high order volume during quarter-end |
| **Delhi** | North zone (Delhi NCR, Haryana, UP, Rajasthan) | North-zone fulfillment; procurement coordination for north-zone vendors | Operations, Procurement, Customer Success | Road freight delays, vendor concentration risk, seasonal demand spikes |
| **Bangalore** | South zone – high-volume tech corridor (Bangalore, Mysore, Pune periphery) | High-volume technology client fulfillment; SLA-sensitive enterprise account management | Operations, Logistics, Customer Success | High SLA expectations from tech clients, rapid order volume growth |
| **Hyderabad** | Reporting and back-office hub; serves Telangana, AP | KPI tracking, operations reporting, customer operations support | Finance, Customer Success, Inventory Planning | Reporting latency, inter-branch data reconciliation |

Each branch is managed by a **Branch Operations Lead** who is accountable for day-to-day operational performance, SLA compliance, and cross-team coordination within the branch. Branch Operations Leads report to the Head of Operations.

## Operating Model

The standard order-to-delivery workflow at abc.co proceeds through the following stages:

1. **Order Intake** — Customer orders are received via the customer portal, email, or account manager channels. Orders are logged in the order management system with a unique Order ID, customer account ID, delivery address, and requested delivery date.

2. **Inventory Verification** — The Inventory Planning Team verifies stock availability at the nearest branch. If stock is insufficient, a procurement request may be raised or the order may be allocated from an alternate branch.

3. **Picking and Packing** — The Operations Team at the fulfilling branch prepares the order, including picking, quality checking, and packing. A dispatch confirmation is generated.

4. **Dispatch and Logistics** — The Logistics Team arranges carrier pickup and generates tracking information. Carrier selection is based on delivery zone, order weight, and SLA requirements.

5. **In-Transit Monitoring** — The Operations Team monitors delivery status via the carrier dashboard. Delays are flagged and escalated per the Shipment Delay Escalation SOP (ABC-OPS-SOP-004).

6. **Delivery and Confirmation** — Upon delivery, proof of delivery is recorded. The Customer Success Team is notified for enterprise accounts.

7. **Issue Handling** — Delivery exceptions, damage reports, or fulfillment complaints are logged as incident tickets and resolved by Customer Success in coordination with Operations and Logistics.

8. **Reporting** — Operational and inventory KPIs are compiled by the Hyderabad operations team and reported to Senior Management on a weekly and monthly basis.

## Core Teams

### Operations Team

The Operations Team is responsible for day-to-day order fulfillment, dispatch coordination, and in-transit shipment monitoring across all branches. Key responsibilities include:

- Receiving and processing customer orders from the order management system
- Coordinating with the Inventory Planning Team for stock allocation
- Managing the picking, packing, and quality check workflow
- Monitoring carrier dashboards for shipment status
- Raising escalation tickets for delayed or at-risk shipments
- Maintaining dispatch logs and escalation records

Typical decisions made by the Operations Team include determining order prioritization, raising severity-level escalations, and coordinating between logistics and inventory teams during fulfillment exceptions.

### Logistics Team

The Logistics Team manages all carrier relationships, route planning, and in-transit escalation resolution. Key responsibilities include:

- Carrier selection, onboarding, and performance monitoring
- Route optimization and last-mile coordination
- Resolution of carrier exceptions and delivery failures
- Providing revised ETA confirmations during delay escalations
- Maintaining carrier SLA data and performance reports

The Logistics Manager is the ultimate authority on revised ETAs during shipment escalations. No revised ETA should be communicated to customers without Logistics Manager confirmation unless explicitly approved by the Branch Operations Lead.

### Procurement Team

The Procurement Team manages vendor relationships, purchase order processing, and supply sourcing. Key responsibilities include:

- Receiving and validating procurement requests from operations and inventory teams
- Maintaining the approved vendor list and vendor onboarding records
- Collecting and comparing vendor quotes for purchases above ₹1,00,000
- Routing purchase orders through the approval matrix
- Managing emergency procurement workflows
- Archiving procurement records per policy

The Procurement Team does not have independent financial approval authority. All commitments above ₹25,000 require sign-off as specified in the Procurement Approval and Vendor Purchase Policy (ABC-FIN-POL-007).

### Inventory Planning Team

The Inventory Planning Team manages stock levels, reorder cycles, and inventory health reporting across all branches. Key responsibilities include:

- Monitoring branch-level stock levels against reorder thresholds
- Identifying slow-moving, aging, excess, and dead-stock inventory
- Raising reorder requests and coordinating with Procurement
- Publishing weekly branch inventory reports and monthly KPI dashboards
- Managing inventory data quality in the warehouse management system

The Inventory Planning Team uses standardized KPI definitions as described in the Inventory Performance KPI Guide (ABC-INV-GDE-003).

### Customer Success Team

The Customer Success Team manages customer relationships, incident resolution, and service quality for all enterprise and key accounts. Key responsibilities include:

- Serving as the primary point of contact for enterprise accounts during incidents
- Sending proactive and formal customer communications during shipment delays
- Logging all customer interactions against the relevant Order ID and Incident Ticket ID
- Coordinating service credit reviews with the Finance Team
- Escalating high-severity issues to Account Managers and senior leadership

Customer Success personnel are not authorized to commit to refunds or financial compensation independently. All financial remediation requests must be routed to the Finance Team per the Customer Communication Playbook (ABC-CS-PLY-002).

### Finance Team

The Finance Team is responsible for payment processing, financial controls, procurement approval, and cost governance. Key responsibilities include:

- Approving procurement requests above ₹1,00,000 and issuing purchase orders
- Reviewing and approving service credit requests above ₹5,000
- Processing vendor invoices and managing payment schedules
- Maintaining financial records for audit purposes
- Flagging and reviewing patterns of emergency procurement or threshold-splitting

The Finance Manager holds approval authority for procurement up to ₹5,00,000 and service credits up to ₹25,000. Amounts above these thresholds require CFO or COO involvement.

### Branch Operations Leads

Branch Operations Leads are the senior operational authority at each branch. They are accountable for:

- Day-to-day operational performance against SLA targets
- Escalation decisions for high-severity shipment delays
- Coordinating cross-team responses to operational incidents
- Ensuring compliance with internal SOPs and policies
- Representing branch performance in weekly operations reviews

Branch Operations Leads report to the Head of Operations and are empowered to make operational decisions within the thresholds defined in relevant SOPs and policies.

### COO Office

The COO Office provides executive oversight across all operational functions. The COO is the approval authority for:

- Procurement commitments above ₹15,00,000 (jointly with CFO)
- Service credits above ₹25,000
- Shipment escalations involving critical accounts with delays exceeding 72 hours
- Any exception to standing policy that requires executive authorization

The COO Office is notified of critical-account escalations and high-value procurement decisions. Notifications do not require a response unless action is needed.

## Common Operational Problems

abc.co has experienced rapid growth over the past two years, which has surfaced several recurring operational challenges that internal tools, SOPs, and reporting systems are designed to address.

**Delayed shipments** remain the most frequent source of customer complaints. Delays arise from carrier capacity constraints, warehouse dispatch backlogs, incorrect address data, and weather disruptions. Without a standardized escalation process, delays have historically been under-reported until they become customer-visible.

**Unclear escalation ownership** has resulted in delays going unaddressed because no single team member accepted responsibility. The Shipment Delay Escalation SOP (ABC-OPS-SOP-004) was introduced to assign clear ownership at each delay severity level.

**Aging inventory** accumulates at branches when demand forecasts are inaccurate or procurement quantities are not aligned with actual consumption rates. Aged stock occupies warehouse space and increases write-off risk.

**Stockout risk** occurs when reorder levels are not monitored proactively, particularly for fast-moving SKUs in high-demand branches. Stockouts directly result in order fulfillment failures and SLA breaches.

**Procurement approval delays** occur when requesters submit incomplete documentation, route requests to incorrect approvers, or fail to collect the required number of vendor quotes. These delays cause downstream inventory shortages.

**Customer communication inconsistency** has led to customers receiving informal, uncoordinated updates during incidents. In several cases, support staff made commitments on refunds or delivery timelines without proper authorization.

**Manual KPI reporting** across branches results in inconsistent metric definitions, delayed reporting, and data quality errors. The Inventory KPI Guide (ABC-INV-GDE-003) was introduced to standardize definitions and reporting cadence.

## Knowledge Management Problem

abc.co's operational knowledge is currently distributed across multiple systems and formats, including shared email threads, branch-level spreadsheets, PDF policy documents, messaging channels, and individual employee knowledge. This fragmentation results in:

- New team members receiving inconsistent onboarding information
- Policy updates not propagating reliably to all relevant staff
- Operational decisions being made without awareness of applicable guidelines
- Audit trails being incomplete or located in non-searchable formats

The internal RAG assistant is intended to provide a single, grounded interface through which team members can query operational policies, escalation procedures, KPI definitions, procurement rules, and communication standards — with all answers traceable to authoritative source documents.

## Use Case for Internal Assistant

The document-grounded internal assistant is designed to:

- Answer operational questions from any team member using authoritative internal documentation as the sole source
- Cite the specific section and document that supports each answer
- Decline to answer questions that are not covered by available documents
- Avoid hallucinating policies, thresholds, or approvals that are not documented
- Support audit readiness by providing consistent, policy-aligned answers

The assistant is not a replacement for managerial judgment. Operational decisions that involve exceptions, edge cases, or significant financial implications must still be reviewed by the relevant approval authority.

## Terminology

The following terms are used consistently across all abc.co internal documents:

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Branch Operations Lead** | The senior operational manager at each branch, accountable for day-to-day operations, escalations, and SLA compliance within their branch |
| **Logistics Manager** | The team lead for the Logistics Team, responsible for carrier relationships and in-transit escalation resolution; the authority for revised ETA confirmation |
| **Procurement Request** | A formal internal request to purchase goods or services, submitted through the order management or procurement system, containing business justification, estimated cost, and preferred vendor |
| **Inventory Aging** | The number of days an inventory item has remained in stock since the date of last receipt or last inventory movement, used to assess stock health and write-off risk |
| **Reorder Level** | The minimum stock quantity for a given SKU at a given branch, below which a replenishment review is automatically triggered |
| **Service Credit** | A financial concession issued to a customer in response to a verified SLA breach or service failure, subject to Finance approval above defined thresholds |
| **SLA Breach** | A failure to meet a committed service level, most commonly a delivery deadline, as defined in the applicable customer contract or standard terms |
| **Escalation Ticket** | A formal internal record created in the operations management system to track a service issue, delay, or incident, including owner assignment, status updates, and resolution timestamp |
