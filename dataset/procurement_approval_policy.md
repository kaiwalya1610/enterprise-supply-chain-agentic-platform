## Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for controlling all procurement spending at abc.co. It ensures that all purchases of goods, services, and subscriptions are made with proper business justification, appropriate authorization, and adherence to approved vendor relationships. The policy is designed to:

- Prevent unauthorized or undocumented financial commitments
- Ensure purchases are aligned with operational needs and approved budgets
- Maintain a clear audit trail for all procurement activities
- Protect the company from vendor concentration risk, overpricing, and conflicts of interest
- Ensure emergency purchases are handled efficiently without bypassing financial controls

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors operating on behalf of abc.co who have the ability to initiate, approve, or execute a purchase. Compliance is mandatory. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action and personal financial liability for unauthorized commitments.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all procurement activities at abc.co, including but not limited to:

- Purchase of physical goods (office supplies, pantry goods, cleaning supplies, electronics, furniture, facility consumables)
- Purchase of services (maintenance, transportation, consulting, temporary staffing)
- Software subscriptions and SaaS tools
- Warehouse and operational supplies
- Branch-level operational purchases (utilities, facility maintenance, minor equipment)
- Emergency purchases required to maintain operational continuity

This policy applies uniformly to all branches: Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Hyderabad.

**Out of scope:**

- Payroll and employee compensation (governed by HR policy)
- Inter-company transfers and internal cost allocations
- Customer refunds and service credits (governed by ABC-OPS-SOP-004 and ABC-CS-PLY-002)
- Capital expenditure above ₹50,00,000, which is governed by a separate CapEx Approval Framework reviewed by the CFO and Board

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Procurement Request** | A formal internal request to purchase goods or services, submitted by an authorized requester and containing business justification, estimated cost, preferred vendor, and budget owner sign-off |
| **Purchase Order (PO)** | A formal financial commitment issued by the Finance Department to a vendor, authorizing the supply of goods or services at agreed terms. No purchase commitment may be made without an approved PO, except under documented emergency procedures |
| **Approved Vendor** | A vendor that has completed abc.co's vendor onboarding process, including verification of GST registration, PAN, bank account, and conflict-of-interest declaration, and is listed in the approved vendor registry |
| **Emergency Purchase** | A procurement action initiated outside the standard workflow due to an immediate operational need that cannot wait for the standard approval cycle, subject to retrospective documentation requirements |
| **Budget Owner** | The Department Head or senior manager with accountability for the budget line against which the purchase will be charged |
| **Department Head** | The designated head of an operational or functional department, authorized to approve procurement requests within their department's budget up to the threshold defined in this policy |
| **Capital Expenditure (CapEx)** | Spending on physical assets expected to provide value for more than 12 months, such as warehouse equipment, furniture, or technology hardware |
| **Operating Expense (OpEx)** | Recurring or consumable spending required to maintain day-to-day operations, such as pantry supplies, cleaning materials, and software subscriptions |
| **Three-Quote Requirement** | The obligation to collect written price quotations from at least three separate approved vendors before placing an order above ₹1,00,000, ensuring competitive pricing |

## Procurement Principles

All procurement activities at abc.co must adhere to the following principles:

1. **Business purpose requirement.** Every purchase must have a documented business justification. Purchases made for personal benefit or without a clear operational need are prohibited.

2. **Approved vendor preference.** Purchases must use approved vendors whenever one exists in the relevant product or service category. Using a non-approved vendor requires written justification and Finance Manager sign-off.

3. **Approval before commitment.** Financial commitment to a vendor — whether by verbal agreement, email, purchase order, or contract signature — may not be made before the applicable approval has been obtained. Retroactive approval is not accepted except under documented emergency procedures.

4. **Emergency documentation timeliness.** Emergency purchases that proceed without advance approval must be fully documented within 2 business days of the purchase. Failure to document within this window is treated as a policy breach.

5. **Purchase splitting is prohibited.** Dividing a single procurement need into multiple smaller purchases in order to remain below approval thresholds is strictly prohibited. Procurement Team members or Finance Team members who identify evidence of purchase splitting are required to escalate to the Finance Manager.

6. **Three-quote compliance.** For purchases above ₹1,00,000, three vendor quotes must be obtained and documented before a PO is issued, unless an approved exception applies.

7. **Conflict of interest disclosure.** Any employee with a personal, financial, or familial relationship with a vendor must disclose this relationship before the procurement review and must not participate in the approval decision.

## Approval Threshold Matrix

All procurement requests must be routed to the appropriate approver based on the total value of the purchase commitment. Where a purchase spans multiple invoices or delivery stages, the total committed value is used to determine the applicable threshold.

| Purchase Value | Required Approver | Supporting Documents Required |
|---|---|---|
| Up to ₹25,000 | Team Lead | Procurement request with business justification; vendor invoice or quote |
| ₹25,001 to ₹1,00,000 | Department Head | Procurement request; vendor quote; budget owner sign-off |
| ₹1,00,001 to ₹5,00,000 | Finance Manager | Procurement request; three vendor quotes; budget owner sign-off; vendor onboarding confirmation |
| ₹5,00,001 to ₹15,00,000 | CFO | Procurement request; three vendor quotes; Finance Manager review note; budget code confirmation; business case summary |
| Above ₹15,00,000 | COO and CFO (joint approval required) | Full procurement request; three vendor quotes; Finance Manager review; business case with ROI or operational justification; COO and CFO signatures |

Approval must be documented in writing. Email approval is acceptable for amounts up to ₹5,00,000, provided the email is archived against the purchase record. Above ₹5,00,000, wet or digital signature on the procurement approval form is required.

## Standard Procurement Workflow

The following steps constitute the standard procurement process for all non-emergency purchases:

1. **Requester Creates Procurement Request** — The requesting team member submits a procurement request in the procurement management system, including: description of goods or services, quantity, estimated total cost, urgency level, preferred vendor (if any), and budget line reference.

2. **Budget Owner Reviews Business Justification** — The Budget Owner (Department Head) reviews the request for operational necessity and budget availability. If the request is within budget and justified, the Budget Owner approves it to proceed to vendor validation.

3. **Vendor Validation** — The Procurement Team confirms whether the preferred vendor is on the approved vendor list. If not, the Procurement Team will identify approved alternatives or initiate vendor onboarding if no approved alternative exists.

4. **Quote Collection** — For purchases above ₹1,00,000, the Procurement Team collects a minimum of three written quotes from eligible vendors. Quotes must be current (issued within the past 30 days) and must specify the goods or services, quantity, unit price, total price, GST, and payment terms.

5. **Approval Routing** — The Procurement Team routes the request to the appropriate approver per the Approval Threshold Matrix. The approver reviews the business justification, vendor selection rationale, and quote comparison before issuing approval.

6. **Purchase Order Creation** — Upon approval, the Finance Department creates and issues a formal Purchase Order to the selected vendor. No verbal or informal commitments may be made to the vendor before PO issuance.

7. **Goods Receipt or Service Confirmation** — Upon delivery of goods or completion of services, the receiving team member confirms receipt in the procurement system, records any discrepancies, and uploads proof of delivery or service completion.

8. **Invoice Matching** — The Finance Team matches the vendor invoice against the approved PO and goods receipt record. Discrepancies of more than 2% or ₹2,000 (whichever is lower) are flagged for resolution before payment.

9. **Payment Processing** — Upon successful invoice matching, the Finance Team schedules payment per the agreed vendor payment terms. Payment is not made to vendors not listed in the approved vendor registry.

10. **Record Archival** — All procurement documents (procurement request, quotes, approval records, PO, delivery confirmation, invoice, and payment record) are archived in the Finance Department's document management system for a minimum of 5 years.

## Vendor Onboarding

No purchase may be made from a new vendor until the vendor has completed the abc.co onboarding process. Vendor onboarding requires the following:

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| GST Registration Certificate | Valid and current; must match vendor's invoicing entity |
| PAN Card | Verified against company name or proprietor name |
| Bank Account Details | Account number, IFSC code, and bank name; verified via penny-drop or bank statement |
| Authorized Contact Person | Name, designation, mobile number, and official email |
| Service or Product Category | Must align with abc.co's procurement categories |
| Payment Terms Agreement | Standard terms: Net 30 days unless otherwise approved by Finance Manager |
| Conflict-of-Interest Declaration | Signed declaration from abc.co's relationship owner confirming no personal interest in the vendor |

Vendor onboarding is managed by the Procurement Team. Onboarded vendors are listed in the approved vendor registry maintained by the Finance Department. The registry is reviewed quarterly to remove vendors with no activity in the past 12 months or with unresolved compliance issues.

## Emergency Procurement

Emergency procurement procedures apply when an immediate purchase is required to prevent operational shutdown, a customer-impacting incident, a safety issue, or a forced branch closure. The following rules govern emergency purchases:

- Emergency procurement is permitted only when the standard approval cycle cannot be completed in time to address the operational need.
- Verbal approval from a Department Head (for amounts up to ₹5,00,000) or CFO (for amounts above ₹5,00,000) must be obtained before proceeding. The name of the approver, the time of verbal approval, and the nature of the emergency must be documented immediately by the requester.
- Full documentation, including the procurement request, vendor details, quote (even informal), invoice, and written confirmation of the verbal approver's authorization, must be submitted within **2 business days** of the emergency purchase.
- Emergency purchases above ₹5,00,000 require CFO review within **1 business day** of the purchase, even if verbal approval was obtained from a lower authority.
- Recurring emergency purchases from the same branch or team (more than 3 instances in a 90-day period) will trigger an audit review by the Finance Manager to assess whether the procurement planning process requires improvement.

## Three-Quote Requirement

For all purchases with a total committed value above ₹1,00,000, the Procurement Team must obtain written quotations from at least three separate eligible vendors before PO issuance.

Exceptions to the three-quote requirement are permitted only in the following cases, each of which requires Finance Manager written approval:

- **Sole-source vendor:** The required good or service is available from only one supplier in the relevant geography or category, and this is documented.
- **Existing master service agreement:** The purchase is covered by an existing MSA with agreed pricing, and the MSA was executed following a competitive process.
- **Emergency purchase:** The operational urgency prevents collection of three quotes. At least one quote must be obtained. The emergency must be documented per the Emergency Procurement rules above.
- **Approved subscription renewal:** The renewal of a previously approved software subscription or service contract at the same or lower cost, where switching vendors would incur disproportionate transition costs.
- **Regulatory or safety purchase:** A specific product is required by applicable regulation or certified safety standard, and no equivalent alternative is approved.

## Prohibited Practices

The following practices are strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action, reimbursement demands, or termination:

- **Purchase splitting:** Dividing a single procurement requirement into smaller transactions to avoid approval thresholds.
- **Personal reimbursement without pre-approval:** Making personal expenditures and seeking reimbursement without advance written approval from the applicable approver.
- **Use of unapproved vendors without justification:** Engaging a vendor not on the approved vendor list without written exception approval from the Finance Manager.
- **Retroactive approvals (non-emergency):** Seeking approval after a commitment has already been made, outside of documented emergency circumstances.
- **Informal commitments via email or chat:** Communicating a financial commitment to a vendor via informal channels without an approved PO. Informal communications do not constitute a valid commitment and expose the company to contractual risk.
- **Conflict of interest non-disclosure:** Failing to disclose a personal, financial, or familial relationship with a vendor involved in a procurement decision.

---

## Exceptions

The following exceptions to standard procurement procedures are recognized. All exceptions require written documentation and the applicable approval:

| Exception | Condition | Required Approval |
|---|---|---|
| Sole-source vendor | Only one eligible vendor exists for the required good or service | Finance Manager |
| Urgent branch outage | Operational shutdown imminent; delay would cause measurable customer or revenue impact | Department Head (verbal), followed by Finance Manager documentation within 2 business days |
| Regulated safety purchase | A specific product is mandated by safety regulation | Department Head; Finance notification required |
| Existing MSA | Purchase is covered by an active, competitively procured master service agreement | Finance Manager to confirm MSA validity |
| Subscription renewal | Renewal of previously approved subscription at same or lower cost | Finance Manager; no additional quotes required if cost has not increased |

## Recordkeeping

The following documents must be retained for all procurement transactions and must be archived in the Finance Department's document management system:

- Original procurement request (with business justification and requester details)
- Budget owner approval email or signature
- Vendor onboarding confirmation (for new vendors)
- All vendor quotations received (minimum three for purchases above ₹1,00,000)
- Quote comparison summary (for purchases above ₹1,00,000)
- Approval record per the Approval Threshold Matrix (email or signed form)
- Issued Purchase Order
- Goods receipt note or service completion confirmation
- Vendor invoice
- Invoice matching record
- Payment confirmation
- Any exception approval documentation

All records must be retained for a minimum of **5 years** from the date of the transaction. Records related to active vendor contracts must be retained for the duration of the contract plus 3 years.

## Examples

### Example 1: ₹18,000 Printer Supplies (Standard Purchase)

An Operations Coordinator at the Bangalore branch needs to purchase printer paper and toner cartridges totaling ₹18,000. The Team Lead reviews the procurement request and vendor quote and approves. No PO is issued for amounts below ₹25,000, but the invoice is archived. No quotes are required beyond the selected vendor invoice as the amount is below ₹1,00,000.

### Example 2: ₹80,000 Warehouse Shelf Repair (Department Head Approval)

The Branch Operations Lead at Delhi raises a procurement request for warehouse shelving repair totaling ₹80,000. The Department Head reviews the business justification and approves. As the amount is below ₹1,00,000, only one vendor quote is required, though comparative quotes are encouraged. Finance creates the PO and schedules payment on Net 30 terms.

### Example 3: ₹3,50,000 Packaging Material Order (Finance Manager Approval)

The Mumbai branch Procurement Team raises a ₹3,50,000 request for bulk procurement of packing materials from an approved supplier. Three vendor quotes are collected and compared. The Finance Manager reviews the quote comparison, confirms budget availability, and approves. A PO is issued. Goods receipt is confirmed upon delivery.

### Example 4: ₹18,00,000 Annual Logistics Software Renewal (COO and CFO Joint Approval)

The Finance Department initiates a renewal for the annual logistics and route planning software subscription at ₹18,00,000. This exceeds the CFO-only threshold. A business case is prepared, and the COO and CFO both sign the approval form. Finance issues the PO to the software vendor. As this is an approved subscription renewal, the three-quote requirement is waived per the Subscription Renewal exception, subject to Finance Manager confirmation.

### Example 5: Emergency Generator Rental (Emergency Procurement)

A power outage at the Hyderabad back-office causes a branch operations shutdown. The Branch Operations Lead verbally approves a rental of a diesel generator for ₹45,000 from a local supplier to restore operations. This is documented immediately by the requester: approver name, time of verbal approval, and nature of emergency. The Procurement Team submits the full documentation package within 2 business days, including the vendor invoice and emergency justification. Finance Manager reviews and closes the record.
